南锣鼓巷 

Nanluoguxiang is one of the oldest neighborhoods in Beijing, with a total length of 786 meters and is listed among 25 old city reserves.
It is the only district in China that has preserved intact the hutong courtyard structure of the Yuan Dynasty, and is the largest, highest-grade, and richest in traditional residential areas of the chessboard style.
-The area is now home to a wide variety of stores with different styles, and a variety of sophisticated and fashionable private houses, making it a fashionable landmark in Beijing.
-For a quiet experience of old Beijing, there are several hutongs on either side of the city, with many famous residences and historical buildings to make your visit worthwhile.
Time reference: more than 3 hours
Transportation: Metro: You can take Metro Line 6 or Line 8 and get off at Nanluoguxiang Station, which is within walking distance.
Opening hours: 10:00-21:00 (all day) (Jan. 1-Dec. 31, Mon.-Sun.)


胡大饭馆

Hu Da Restaurant is a Sichuan cuisine based restaurant with a classical Chinese architecture, which is very grand.
The signature spicy crawfish and frog are almost a must-try, so fresh and fatty that you can't stop.
Business is always brisk here, so be prepared to wait in line to dine.
Address
No.233 Dongzhimennei Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing


四季民福烤鸭店

Four Seasons Minfu Roast Duck Restaurant is located near the East Gate of the Forbidden City and is a Beijing-style restaurant specializing in roast duck.
The restaurant has a great environment and attentive service, and its best feature is its view of the East Gate and the Horn Tower.
The duck is roasted in the old-fashioned way, melting in your mouth, dipped in garlic paste, crispy and fragrant, and the traditional Beijing pastries are also very authentic.
There is a queue for the main dining time and a longer wait for the view table.
Address
No.11 Nanchizi Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing



东来顺

Dong Laishun, a long-established Chinese restaurant, was founded in 1903 and has now opened in all the streets of Beijing.
Dong Lai Shun is a halal restaurant with a traditional large copper pot, a red-hot, roaring charcoal fire, and carefully selected lamb meat.
The fresh lamb rolls are rolled up and down in the pot several times, and then dipped in a mixture of sesame sauce, leek flowers and curd, which warms the heart and stomach.
Address
No.198 Wangfujing Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing
